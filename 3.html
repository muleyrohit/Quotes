If you have more than one reason to do something (choose a doctor or a veternarian, hire a gardener or an employee, marry a person, go on a trip), just don't do it. It doesn't mean that one reason is better than two, just that by invoking more tham one reason you are trying to convince yourself to do something. Obvious decisions (robust to error) require no more than a single reason.
~ Nassim Nicholas Taleb.
